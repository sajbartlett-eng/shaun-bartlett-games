The Framework

Every game reveals the same underlying pattern: individual differences + systemic incentives = emergent behavior. No two games teach the same lesson, but all follow the same design principles.

Asymmetric by Design

Players don't start equal. Different abilities, different constraints, different incentives. Inequality isn't a bug‚Äîit's how systems actually work.

Mechanics as Incentives

Scoring rules create behavior. Want cooperation? Reward it structurally. Want tragedy? Make selfishness individually rational.

Emergence Over Instruction

No one tells you "cooperation good, hoarding bad." You discover it through play. The lesson emerges from experiencing consequences.

Real Stakes, Safe Space

Games create genuine tension and difficult choices‚Äîbut consequences stay in the game. Learn from failure without real-world cost.

Core Mission: Systems Literacy

Most people don't understand how systems work. They see outcomes and blame individuals. They miss the incentive structures that create behavior.

These games make systems visible. Not through explanation‚Äîthrough experience. Play them and you'll understand why rational actors produce irrational outcomes. Why good people create broken systems. Why changing individuals doesn't change results unless you change the rules.
