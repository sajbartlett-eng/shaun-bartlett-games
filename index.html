The Systems Literacy Framework

Eight core principles that translate any complex human system into experiential learning.

1. Asymmetric Interdependence

  • ‚Ä¢ Different abilities/constraints per role
  • ‚Ä¢ Shared fate despite unequal power
  • ‚Ä¢ Individual success depends on system health
Examples: Winter Den (Bear vs Rabbit capacities), Tipping Point (CEO role differences), Summer Den (same events, different ME costs), Leverage (King vs Noble treasuries)

2. Mechanical Honesty

  • ‚Ä¢ Scoring encodes the real incentive structure
  • ‚Ä¢ Rules mirror reality, not idealized version
  • ‚Ä¢ The math teaches the lesson
Examples: Winter Den (¬±1 flips based on survival), Tipping Point (Crisis accelerates at threshold 5), Burnout (promotion only if team passes)

3. Cascading Consequences

  • ‚Ä¢ Choices compound over time
  • ‚Ä¢ One action ripples through system
  • ‚Ä¢ Path dependency matters
Examples: Tipping Point (deregulation gets worse), Viral (updates build on updates), Burnout (one burnout increases others' pressure), Winter Den (early excess creates late scarcity)

4. Pacing Creates Pressure

  • ‚Ä¢ Urgency forces real-time decisions
  • ‚Ä¢ No time for perfect optimization
  • ‚Ä¢ Constraint reveals authentic choice patterns
Examples: Burnout (3-5 min timer), Viral (30-60 sec turns), Inheritance (60 sec challenge), Tipping Point (bankruptcy risk), Winter Den (8 rounds)

5. Emergence Over Instruction

  • ‚Ä¢ Players discover the lesson through play
  • ‚Ä¢ No explanation of "the point"
  • ‚Ä¢ System teaches itself via consequences
Examples: Winter Den (never says "excess bad"), Tipping Point (feel collapse approaching), Viral (watch truth degrade), Burnout (experience toxic incentives)

6. Narrative Engines

  • ‚Ä¢ Mechanics generate stories, not scripts
  • ‚Ä¢ Each playthrough creates unique arc
  • ‚Ä¢ Player choices write the narrative
Examples: Winter Den (Rabbit's fate emerges), Leverage (betrayal timing matters), Tipping Point (who triggers collapse varies)

7. Multiple Valid Strategies

  • ‚Ä¢ Different approaches can work
  • ‚Ä¢ "Optimal" play varies by context
  • ‚Ä¢ Reflects real-world strategic diversity
Examples: Inheritance (cluster vs spread assets), Tipping Point (cooperation vs calculated risk vs aggressive gambling), Leverage (three victory paths), Winter Den (efficiency vs collaboration vs isolation)

8. Maximum Accessibility

  • ‚Ä¢ Minimal components
  • ‚Ä¢ Quick to learn, quick to play
  • ‚Ä¢ Low barrier to entry
Examples: Viral (paper + pen), Leverage (standard deck), Burnout (bag + cubes), digital games (browser, no download)

Core Innovation

The same methodology that teaches basketball court awareness also teaches empathy, moral reasoning, and systems thinking. Any complex human skill can be translated into experiential learning.
