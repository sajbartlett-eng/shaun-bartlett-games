The Mechanic: The Budget Cliff. Each Quarter must hit exactly 20. If you are under, the group continues. If you go over, the Quarter closes, and you (the player who played the last card) are now the Scapegoat.

The Insight: The system forces you to push your luck. You want to contribute to the budget to empty your hand, but you never want to be the one who finishes it‚Äîunless it's perfect.

The Mechanic: The Memory. Players can play cards "Face Down" as Fraud to close a quarter quickly. These cards stay on the table until the end of the year.

The Corporate Ladder

Your hand size represents your political capital.

4

Junior Staff

Starting State. You have few options and limited flexibility. You are frequently forced to play cards that hurt you.
5

Middle Management

The Transition. You have more capacity to hold Aces and high cards, but you are still one mistake away from being demoted back to the bottom.
6

Executive

Victory Condition. If you reach Hand Size 6, you win immediately. You have stopped managing the budget and started setting it.

The Pit

There is no elimination in Scapegoat, only humiliation. If you are Demoted below Hand Size 4, you drop to Hand Size 1. You have zero choices. You must play whatever card you draw. You are entirely at the mercy of the system until you can claw your way back.
