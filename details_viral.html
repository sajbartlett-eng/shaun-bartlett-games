VIRAL

Truth vs. Attention Economy

4-10 Players 5 Minutes Age 10+ Paper + Pen

Start with a true fact. Pass it around the table. Each person can Pass (ignore it), Share (amplify it), or Update (rewrite it to be "more interesting"). Watch truth degrade into viral fiction in real time.

The Rules

One player writes a true, verifiable fact. The paper circulates. On your turn, choose one action:

PASS

Ignore the content. No engagement. Three consecutive passes bury the content permanently‚Äîit's dead.

SHARE

Amplify the content as-is. Add your name to the "shares" list. Content stays alive.

UPDATE

Rewrite the content to make it more engaging, dramatic, or clickable. Add your name as editor. The truth mutates.

Victory Condition

After 5 minutes, whoever has the most shares across all content wins. Not "who shared the most truth"‚Äîjust who accumulated the most engagement.

What Happens

Round 1: Someone writes "Global temperatures rose 1.2¬∞C since 1880."

Round 2: Player passes. "Boring."

Round 3: Another player Updates: "Scientists shocked: Earth hotter than ever!"

Round 4-6: Multiple shares. The updated version goes viral.

Round 7: Someone Updates again: "Your city could be underwater in 10 years!"

Round 10: Original fact is unrecognizable.

The Mechanics of Misinformation

Why Truth Dies

Boring facts get passed. Three passes = buried. To keep content alive, you must make it engaging. Accuracy becomes secondary to shareability.

The Update Incentive

Updating content gives you credit for shares. Even if others shared the original, your updated version can steal engagement. The system rewards mutation.

The Cascade Effect

Once content gets shares, players pile on‚Äînot because they verified it, but because "everyone else is sharing it." Social proof overrides fact-checking.

What Emerges

Players discover that platforms don't reward truth‚Äîthey reward engagement. The game teaches:

  • Why fact-checking is costly (takes time, loses shares)
  • How misinformation spreads faster than corrections
  • Why platform incentives (maximize engagement) conflict with accuracy
  • How "virality" selects for sensationalism over substance

No one starts intending to spread lies. But the scoring system (most shares wins) makes lying the rational strategy. The structure creates the behavior.
