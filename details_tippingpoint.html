You are an industry titan. The planet is just collateral.

Every turn, you must invest in one of three markets using a volatility engine (2d6). Your goal is to be the richest player at the table. However, if the group fails to save the environment, everyone loses.

The Central Conflict

Pollution pays. The "Deregulation" market offers the highest returns but accelerates the Crisis. "Green Tech" saves the game but pays poverty wages. You must rely on someone else to save the world so you can get rich.

System Design Analysis

How the Systems Literacy Framework is applied to climate economics.

‚öñÔ∏è Structural Tension

The Mechanic: The Tragedy of the Commons. To win the game, the group must cooperate. To win the table, you must defect.

The Insight: Rational individual behavior (maximizing ROI) leads to collective suicide. The game proves that climate collapse isn't caused by villains; it's caused by math.

Individual incentives are at war with system stability.

üîó Asymmetric Interdependence

The Mechanic: The Finance Role. One player produces nothing. They only bet on others and issue loans.

The Insight: Finance extracts wealth from the risk taken by others. The banker needs the industries to be successful enough to pay interest, but desperate enough to need loans.

Models the parasitic relationship between capital and production.

‚öôÔ∏è Mechanical Honesty

The Mechanic: The Secure Action. Players can pay cash to lower their dice target number.

The Insight: Risk is for the poor. The rich can buy certainty. The mechanic demonstrates that market volatility only destroys those who cannot afford to hedge against it.

Money buys probability.
The "Secure" interface visualizes the cost of certainty. Players with wealth can buy better odds, while poorer players are forced to rely on raw luck.

Rules Overview

The Three Markets

Success relies on rolling 2d6 against dynamic target numbers.

DEREGULATION

High Risk, High Reward. Pays massively ($4-$6) but increases Crisis. Success makes this market more volatile.

CONVENTIONAL

The Safe Bet. Moderate pay. Still generates pollution. The path of least resistance.

GREEN TECH

The Only Way Out. Low pay ($2). Increases Env Health. If the group doesn't invest here, everyone dies.

Asymmetric Roles

The game features 5 distinct roles, including The Finance Sector (who plays a completely different mini-game of betting and lending) and Tech Startup (who makes Green Tech cheaper for everyone else).

Win Conditions

  • WIN Planet Saved: Fill the Environment Track to 5. The richest player wins.
  • LOSE Collapse: The Crisis Track hits the limit. Everyone loses immediately.
