The Three-Phase Game

Phase 1: Develop

Players take turns placing assets from a shared pool onto a 6√ó6 grid. The goal is to create clusters of value that are difficult to separate. Every empty square is worth 1 point (Land Value).

Phase 2: The Cut

One player is assigned the role of Cutter (via Rock, Paper, Scissors). Under a 60-second timer, the Cutter must draw a fence line from edge-to-edge, dividing the estate into two distinct plots. The goal is to make the split as equal as possible (a tie), or to set a trap.

Phase 3: The Choice

The other player, the Chooser, selects which of the two plots they want to keep. The Cutter gets whatever is left.

Mechanic: The Fairness Award

If the Chooser wins by a significant margin (>1 point), the game challenges them to prove their skill. They are given 30 seconds to redraw the fence to make the game perfectly even (‚â§1 point difference). Success grants the "Fair Sibling" accolade.

Design Philosophy

Inheritance gamifies the mathematical concept of Fair Division. It forces players to confront the computational difficulty of the Partition Problem in real-time. When you are the Cutter, you are fighting against your own greed; create a split that is too uneven, and your opponent will simply take the better half.
