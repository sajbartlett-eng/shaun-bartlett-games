INHERITANCE

Fair Division & Game Theory

2 Players 10-15 Minutes Age 12+ Digital

Two players inherit an estate filled with valuable assets. The catch? You must divide it fairly—or prove that fair division is impossible.

The Three-Phase Game

Phase 1: Placement

Player 1 (the Placer) strategically places assets—Gold (5 pts), Silver (3 pts), Green (2 pts), Red (1 pt)—and Rocks (obstacles) onto a 5×5 grid. The goal: make equal division as difficult as possible.

Phase 2: Division

Player 2 (the Divider) must draw a fence line that splits the estate into exactly two plots. The fence must run edge-to-edge and create valid partitions. Under time pressure, find the most equitable split you can.

Phase 3: Choice

The Divider chooses their preferred plot. Scores are calculated. Winner determined... unless.

The Fairness Challenge

If the score difference exceeds 1 point, the Placer can challenge. They get 60 seconds to find a MORE fair division (≤1 point difference). If they succeed, the Placer wins. If they fail or time expires, the original winner stands.

What Makes It Work

The Fairness Challenge mechanic embodies a simple principle: if you claim a problem is unfair, you must demonstrate a fairer solution.

This isn't just clever game design—it's teaching the Partition Problem (NP-complete in computer science) through play. Players viscerally experience why finding optimal fair divisions is computationally hard.

Optional: Prime Bonus

Adjacent Green tiles get +3 bonus points each. This creates spatial synergy and makes asymmetric value clusters more powerful—forcing the Divider to choose between connected high-value zones.

Learning Through Failure

The game teaches:

  • Fair division requires constructive proof, not just complaints
  • Optimal solutions exist but are hard to find under pressure
  • Strategic problem design can make "fair" nearly impossible
  • Approximation algorithms matter when perfect solutions are expensive
