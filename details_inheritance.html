Inheritance

Estate Edition
2 Players (Local) ~3 Mins HTML5 Canvas Touch Optimized

Two siblings. One Estate. A battle of wits using the classic "Cut and Choose" protocol. This is a digital implementation of the fair division problem, forcing players to confront the mathematical difficulty of partitioning value in real-time.

The Three-Phase Loop

Phase 1: Develop (Unknown Roles)

Players alternate turns placing assets from a limited shared pool onto a 6√ó6 grid. Crucially, neither player knows their role yet. You must build value without knowing if you will be the one dividing it or the one choosing it.

Phase 2: The Cut

Once the estate is fully built, roles are assigned via Rock, Paper, Scissors. The loser becomes the Cutter. Under a 60-second timer, they must draw a fence line from edge-to-edge, dividing the estate into two distinct plots. The goal is to make the split as equal as possible to prevent the opponent from getting a better deal.

Phase 3: The Choice

The winner of RPS becomes the Chooser. They survey the two plots and select the one with the highest total value (Area + Assets). The Cutter is left with the remainder.

Mechanic: The Fairness Challenge

If the Chooser exploits a bad cut and wins by >1 point, the game offers a redemption challenge. They must redraw the fence to make the plots perfectly even (‚â§1 point difference) within 30 seconds to win the "Fair Sibling" award.

Technical Highlight: Deterministic Solver

The game features a "Show Ideal Cut" button at the end of the match. This triggers a depth-first search algorithm (`performSmartSearch`) that analyzes the grid state to find the mathematically optimal fence line that minimizes the difference between the two plots, proving whether a fair cut was possible.
