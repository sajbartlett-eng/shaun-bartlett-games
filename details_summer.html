The same event. A completely different experience.

Five animals navigate summer in a shared community. They must collect Joy, Connection, and Growth to flourish. But there is a catch: The same "Party" that recharges the Raccoon will drain the Mole. Success depends on recognizing that everyone experiences the world differently.

The Core Challenge

To flourish, you must meet your specific emotional goals. But energy is finite. If any animal ends in Burnout (Mental Energy drops below minimum), everyone loses their bonus points. You cannot succeed if your community is exhausted.

System Design Analysis

How the Systems Literacy Framework is applied to neurodiversity.

üîó Asymmetric Interdependence

The Mechanic: Subjective Reality. Events resolve differently based on personality. A Large Group (+3 Animals) gives Energy to the Raccoon but costs Energy for the Rabbit.

The Insight: Neurodiversity is structural. There is no "fun" event; there is only the interaction between a nervous system and an environment. Forcing the Mole to "have fun" at a party is technically an attack.

‚öôÔ∏è Mechanical Honesty

The Mechanic: Host Tokens. You can spend your own Mental Energy to reduce the negative impact of an event on others.

The Insight: Emotional Labor has a cost. The game validates the invisible work of inclusion. Making a space safe for others requires you to spend your own resources.

Kindness is a resource that depletes.

‚öñÔ∏è Structural Tension

The Mechanic: Collective Burnout. If one player burns out, the group fails the bonus objective.

The Insight: You cannot flourish in a sick system. The "strong" (high energy) players must protect the "weak" (low energy) players, not out of charity, but because their own success is contractually tied to the community's health.
