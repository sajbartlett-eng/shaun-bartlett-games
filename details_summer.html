SUMMER DEN

A Social Dynamics Game Where Different Personalities Experience Events Differently

2-5 Players 20-30 Minutes Age 8+ Print & Play

Five animals navigate summer in a shared community, moving between rooms to join activities. The same event affects each animal completely differently based on their personality, group size, and social preferences. The goal is to meet your animal's unique Flourishing Goals while managing your Mental Energy.

Core Mechanics

Mental Energy Management

Every action costs or restores Mental Energy. Participating in events, arguing to change them, or moving to different rooms all have costs that vary by personality. If your final ME drops below your minimum threshold, you're in Burnout‚Äîand everyone loses bonus points.

Host Tokens (üç™)

A new mechanic for making large gatherings work. After placing or joining an event, players can spend ME to add Host Tokens‚Äîbut only if 3+ animals are expected. Each Host Token:

  • Reduces negative ME impact by 1
  • Unlocks bonus tokens marked with (*) on animal cards
  • Represents emotional labor‚Äîmaking large gatherings welcoming to different temperaments
  • Disappears if the event is replaced via ARGUE action

Override & Move Actions

On your turn during Social Dynamics phase, you can:

  • Argue (Replace Event): Pay ME to swap the event card with a different one. Bear argues cheaply (1 ME), Rabbit struggles (4 ME). All Host Tokens vanish when an event is replaced.
  • Argue (Prepare to Counter): Pay ME now to stay active, anticipating someone might change your event. Keeps you in the round.
  • Move: Pay ME to relocate to a different room with a different event. Mole moves easily (1 ME), Bear is anchored (3 ME).
  • Host: Spend any amount of ME to add that many Host Tokens to your current event (only works if 3+ animals expected).
  • Pass: Accept the event as-is. You're satisfied and can't take more actions this round.

Group Size Effects

Events resolve differently based on how many animals participate:

  • Solo (1): Alone. Some personalities thrive (Fox, Mole on Reflection), others struggle (Bear, Raccoon).
  • Small (2): Intimate gathering. Ideal for Arts & Crafts and Support Circle. Balanced outcomes for most.
  • Large (3+): Community event. Extroverts gain massive benefits; introverts deplete. Host Tokens become essential for protecting introverts.

Victory Conditions

Individual Win: After 5 rounds, meet all three of your Flourishing Goals (token requirements) while maintaining minimum ME threshold. Each goal met = +3 points.

Excess Tokens: Any tokens beyond your requirements count as +1 point each‚Äîbut only if no one burned out.

Community Burnout Penalty: If any animal ends with ME below their minimum (Burnout), all surviving animals subtract their excess tokens instead of adding them. Hoarding while others suffer costs you.

What Emerges

Personality Recognition

The game teaches empathy mechanically. The Bear learns that pushing for large group events exhausts the Mole‚Äîunless someone hosts. The Raccoon discovers that constant event-changing stresses the Rabbit. Success requires noticing what different personalities actually need.

The Hosting Dilemma

Host Tokens represent emotional labor. When you host a Party or Exploration, you spend your own ME to make the event welcoming for introverts. Multiple animals can co-host, sharing the cost and creating protection. But hosting requires sacrifice‚Äîand not everyone can afford it.

Accommodation Decisions

Do you spend precious ME moving to accommodate someone else's needs? The Mole can easily relocate (1 ME) to support others. The Bear must choose whether paying 3 ME to move is worth helping someone else flourish. Or do you argue for what you need and force others to adapt?

The Burnout Threat

The shared penalty for burnout creates collective responsibility without removing individual goals. You can't force others to rest, but you benefit from noticing when someone is depleting and adjusting your actions‚Äîhosting events, moving to join them, or arguing less‚Äîto keep everyone above their threshold.
