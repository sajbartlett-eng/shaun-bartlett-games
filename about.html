SHAUN BARTLETT

Systems Engineer & Game Designer
"I was told basketball intelligence was impossible to teach—that it was a gift you either had or you didn't. I spent the next 15 years proving that wrong."

My journey into game design didn't start with a love of board games. It started with a rejection. At 17, a national tournament coach cut me from the squad, telling me I "wasn't smart enough" to play at higher levels.

That moment sparked an obsession: Is intuition teachable?

I became an engineer specializing in risk-based systems strategy. For years, I fixed broken corporate systems—identifying where complexity was too high, where incentives were misaligned, and where "human error" was actually just bad design. I realized that the root cause of systemic collapse is rarely incompetence; it is poor systems literacy.

The Epiphany

One day, the two worlds collided. I realized that the spatial navigation of basketball and the risk management of engineering were the same problem. And if they were systems, they could be modeled.

I spent three years developing Playmaker, a tool to make basketball IQ experiential. In doing so, I uncovered the Systems Literacy Framework—a set of 8 design engines that can translate complex human friction into playable simulations.

From Theory to Generative Engine

Once the framework existed, the applications exploded. I turned my attention to my own children. My daughter needed to understand fairness, so Winter Den emerged to model survival ethics. She needed to navigate neurodiversity at school, so Summer Den was born to model mental energy costs.

From there, the framework proved it was platform-agnostic. A standard deck of cards became a feudal power simulation (*Leverage*). Two dice became a climate economy model (*The Tipping Point*). A piece of paper became a lesson in algorithmic bias (*Viral*).

I am now mapping a full curriculum of human systems. My goal is to hand these tools to educators, parents, and leaders to make the invisible mechanics of our world visible, playable, and solvable.
