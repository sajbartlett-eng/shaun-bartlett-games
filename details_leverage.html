LEVERAGE

A Card Game of Power, Gold, and Betrayal

4-10 Players 15-30 Minutes Age 12+ Standard Deck

The old King is dead. The Realm lies scattered and in chaos. Using only a standard deck of playing cards, players must use diplomacy, warfare, and betrayal to rise from the ashes. It is Game of Thrones in 20 minutes.

Setup & The Rise to Power

There are no permanent rulers at the start. Every player begins the game as a Mercenary with a hand of only 3 cards. To gain power, you must navigate the Political Phase to become a Noble or seize a crown to become a King.

Three Victory Paths

The Winning King

Be the final ruler crowned. Survive the "War of Kings" or politically unify the realm by holding the allegiance of all other players.

The Wealthiest Individual

Accumulate the most Gold cards (2-10) in your hand when the game ends. Even a Mercenary can win if they are rich enough.

The Loyal Noble

Be a Noble holding Allegiance to the Winning King when the game ends. Sometimes the smartest play is to serve the strongest master.

Core Mechanics

Status & Treasury

Your power is defined by your Treasury‚Äîthe maximum number of cards you can hold in your hand. If you exceed this limit, you must discard immediately.

  • Mercenary (Treasury 3): You serve no one. Your goal is to be hired or seize power.
  • Noble (Treasury 5): You have pledged Allegiance to a King. You provide them military strength.
  • King (Treasury 7): You hold the loyalty of others, but you are the target of every Challenger.

Leverage Cards

During the Political Phase, players use face cards to manipulate the game:

  • Jacks (The Threat): Initiate a Challenge against a King to steal their crown.
  • Red Queens (Cunning): Draw 4 cards to rapidly cycle through the deck and find Gold.
  • Black Queens (Blackmail): Force a target player to hand over their highest value Gold card.

Battle & The War of Kings

Conflict is resolved through bidding hidden Gold. Players may Yield before cards are revealed to save their Gold, or fight to the death. In the endgame War of Kings, diplomacy ends. Kingdoms clash until only one remains, and Nobles must decide: support their King with gold, or watch them die to trigger a succession crisis.

What Emerges

The game creates a distinct tension between the three victory paths. To be the Winning King, you must spend Gold to win battles. To be the Wealthiest Individual, you must hoard Gold and avoid conflict. To be a loyal Noble, you must bet on the right horse‚Äîand be willing to betray them if they stumble.
