BURNOUT

A Semi-Cooperative Game of Collective Pressure

2-5 Players 3-5 Minutes Age 12+ Bag Building

Collaboration is mandatory. Success is individual.

You are a team working to complete a project before a tight deadline. Everyone draws from the same shared bag of tokens. Work too hard, and you burn out. Rest too much, and the project fails.

But there is a twist: Only the highest scoring player gets the Promotion. However, if the team fails to meet the minimum Project Quality (Grade C), no one gets the promotion.

The Corporate Dilemma

You must work hard enough to beat your teammates for the promotion, but not so hard that you burn out‚Äîand you must ensure your teammates work hard enough to pass the group grade, but not so hard that they burn out and drag you down with them.

Five Actions

On your turn, you must choose one:

  • Work: Draw from the bag. Green/Yellow tokens score points. Red tokens increase Stress.
  • Rest: Remove 2 Stress tokens (one is removed from the game, one returns to the bag to pollute it).
  • Delegate: Transfer 1 Stress token from your area to another player's area. (Protection at others' expense).
  • Support: Remove 1 Stress token from another player and discard it permanently. (Requires you to have low stress).
  • Overtime: Take two "Work" actions in a row. High risk, high reward.

Stress & Burnout Mechanics

The Stressed State (3-4 Tokens)

If you have 3 or 4 stress tokens, you are Stressed. You cannot keep any new work tokens you draw (they are discarded), and you lose the emotional capacity to Support others.

The Burnout State (5+ Tokens)

If you hit 5 stress tokens, you Burn Out immediately.

  • Penalty: You must discard 3 Work tokens from your score pile.
  • Restriction: You cannot Work or Support. You can only Rest or Delegate until your stress drops below 5.

Why It Works

The game is structurally designed to create three outcomes:

  1. Success + Success: The team passes, and a high achiever wins.
  2. Sacrifice + Promotion: Some players sacrifice their score to ensure the project passes, allowing a "selfish" player to win.
  3. Failure + Failure: Greed causes the team to crash; nobody wins.

The inclusion of the Delegate action allows players to actively push colleagues toward burnout to save themselves, turning a cooperative puzzle into a social deduction battleground over a span of just 5 minutes.
