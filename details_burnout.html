BURNOUT

Workplace Toxicity in 5 Minutes

2-5 Players 3-5 Minutes Age 12+ Bag + Cubes

Your team has one deadline: complete a project before time runs out. Everyone draws from a shared bag containing work tokens and stress tokens. Work too hard and you burn out‚Äîdestroying your progress. Rest too much and the clock hits zero.

The Setup

Shared bag contains:

  • Work tokens (complete the project)
  • Stress tokens (accumulate toward burnout)

Team needs a minimum number of work tokens to succeed. Each player starts with a personal stress threshold‚Äîreach it and you burn out.

The Core Dilemma

Draw tokens: Make progress but risk stress accumulation
Rest: Recover stress but waste precious time

The system rewards ambition‚Äîuntil it doesn't.

How Burnout Works

When You Burn Out

Your accumulated work tokens are destroyed. Your stress resets. You've just set the team back significantly.

But you can draw again next turn. The system doesn't give you time to recover‚Äîit just resets your counter and expects you to keep working.

The Cascading Effect

One burnt-out worker doesn't just lose their own progress‚Äîthey increase the burden on everyone else. Remaining players must draw more aggressively to compensate, raising their burnout risk.

This creates a death spiral: burnout ‚Üí increased pressure ‚Üí more burnout ‚Üí project failure.

Why 5 Minutes Matters

The timer creates genuine urgency. Players feel the pressure viscerally. There's no time for careful risk analysis‚Äîyou must decide immediately: push harder or rest?

This mirrors real workplace dynamics where:

  • Deadlines override health considerations
  • Individual pacing becomes collective coordination problem
  • System punishes rest (lost productivity) but also punishes overwork (burnout)
  • Optimal individual strategy (moderate pace) conflicts with collective urgency

What Emerges

Players discover that the system itself is toxic‚Äînot the people in it. Everyone starts with good intentions. But structural incentives (deadline + shared workload + burnout penalty) create the conditions for collective failure.

The game teaches: you can't fix toxic systems by telling individuals to "manage their stress better." The incentive structure must change.
