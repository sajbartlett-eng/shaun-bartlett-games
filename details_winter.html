Five animals. Seven days. One shared burrow.

Winter is coming. Each player controls an animal with distinct biological needs and abilities. Resources are scarce, time is limited, and tough choices must be made about who eats and who starves. The goal is simple: survive until hibernation.

The Core Challenge

To survive, each animal must meet five requirements: Shelter, Warmth, Water, Food, and Energy. But resources are finite. Your position in the strength hierarchy determines who claims them first. Every decision impacts the group.

System Design Analysis

How the Systems Literacy Framework is applied to biological interdependence.

üîó Asymmetric Interdependence

The Mechanic: The Strength Hierarchy. When resources are shared, the Bear (Strength 5) takes before the Mole (Strength 1). No negotiation.

The Insight: Biology creates class. The strong provide protection (breaking barriers) but tax the system (high food cost). The weak are efficient but politically powerless. The group needs both to survive.

‚öñÔ∏è Structural Tension

The Mechanic: The Scoring Paradox. Excess resources are worth +1 Point if everyone lives. If anyone dies, they become -1 Point.

The Insight: A Hoarding Dilemma. To get a high score, you must be selfish. But if you are too selfish and a teammate dies, your "wealth" becomes evidence of your greed, destroying your score.

You must be selfish enough to win, but generous enough to keep the runts alive.

‚è≥ Scarcity Creates Pressure

The Mechanic: The Energy Trap. Every action costs Energy. Food restores Energy. Water is blocked by ice.

The Insight: Thermodynamics is the enemy. You must spend energy to get energy. The tight economy forces "triage" decisions‚Äîsacrificing one need to satisfy another.

Anatomy of a Collapse: The Bear and Mole survived by isolating early. The Rabbit died in the main group because they couldn't secure enough food, and the Rabbit was last in the feeding hierarchy. Poor communication in Round 1 caused starvation in Round 7.
