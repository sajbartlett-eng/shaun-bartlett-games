WINTER DEN

A Cooperative Survival Fable Where Individual Choices Have Group Consequences

1-5 Players 20-30 Minutes Age 12+ Print & Play

Five unique animals have just 7 days to prepare for winter hibernation in a shared den. Each player controls one animal with distinct abilities, resource needs, and strategic pressures. Resources are scarce, time is limited, and tough choices must be made about who gets priority access to food, warmth, and shelter.

The Core Challenge

To survive, each animal must meet five requirements: shelter, warmth, water, food, and energy. Resources are limited, and your position in the strength hierarchy determines who claims them first when multiple animals share space. Every decision impacts the entire group.

The Structural Dilemma

Resource Scarcity

The game starts with all water sources blocked by barricades. There are only 20 food tokens and 12 warmth tokens available‚Äînot enough for everyone to thrive. Players must dig for water, build shelter, and decide who gets priority access.

The Strength Hierarchy

When multiple animals share space, the Bear claims first, then Fox, Raccoon, Rabbit, and finally Mole. This creates an implicit caste system where weaker animals must either serve or isolate themselves‚Äîbut isolation means missing out on shared warmth and water.

The Energy Trap

Every action costs energy. Gathering food costs energy. Building barricades costs energy. Even moving resources costs energy. To survive, you must end with enough energy‚Äîbut working too hard to help others might leave you with nothing for yourself.
