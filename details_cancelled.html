You are the Rising Star. They are the Parasites.

To your face, the Media players are all smiles and flashbulbs. But one of them is The Tabloid. They don't just want to use you; they want to end you. However, the Tabloid has a problem: if they destroy you too quickly, they might not have sold enough papers to win.

They need to fatten you up before the slaughter. Can you survive the news cycle long enough to figure out who is holding the knife?

The Predator's Dilemma

The Kill Shot: The Tabloid only wins if they play the 3rd Scandal card AND have the highest score at the table[cite: 116]. [cite_start]If they kill the Star while another player (like the Paparazzi) is richer, the Tabloid loses[cite: 117]. This forces the killer to protect the victim during the early game.

System Design Analysis

How the Systems Literacy Framework is applied to media incentives.

‚öñÔ∏è Structural Tension

The Mechanic: The Fattening Phase. The "Assassin" (Tabloid) behaves exactly like an ally in the early game.

The Insight: Predatory systems often look like supportive ones initially. The Tabloid needs the Star to generate content (points) before the cancellation is profitable. You are not a person; you are an asset class to be liquidated at the peak of the market.

‚öôÔ∏è Mechanical Honesty

[cite_start]

The Mechanic: Silence as a Weapon. When called upon, a player can Publish (Score Points) or Pass (Score 0)[cite: 167, 173].

The Insight: A "Pass" is not just inaction; it is a calculated burial of the truth. You might Pass to save the Star from a Scandal, or you might Pass to deny the Star a Puff Piece. [cite_start]The system rewards silence as much as noise[cite: 178, 180].

üîó Asymmetric Interdependence

The Mechanic: The Interview. The Star has no cards. [cite_start]Their only power is choosing who gets to speak[cite: 163].

The Insight: The subject of the news has no voice, only the power to grant access. By choosing a Media player to "interview," the Star inadvertently hands them the weapon used to destroy them.

Rules Overview

1. The Draft (Editorial Meeting)

[cite_start]

Players are dealt 7 cards but must cut down to a Personal Draw Stack of 5[cite: 153]. [cite_start]This creates a tension between "The Hand You Were Dealt" and "The Hand You Chose"[cite: 155]. You are complicit in the cards you hold.

2. The Choice: Publish or Pass

When the Star chooses you for an Interview, you look at the top card of your stack and make a choice:

  • PUBLISH: Flip the card. [cite_start]You score the points, and the effect applies to the Star[cite: 171].
  • PASS: Discard the card unseen. You score 0 points. [cite_start]You sacrifice profit to control the timing[cite: 177, 178].

3. The End Game

[cite_start]

If the Star survives 10 rounds, they must guess the role of every player[cite: 192].

    [cite_start]
  • All Correct: The Star Wins[cite: 194].
  • Any Wrong: The game enters OVERTIME (Sudden Death). [cite_start]The Tabloid gets another chance to kill the Star every turn[cite: 196, 200].
